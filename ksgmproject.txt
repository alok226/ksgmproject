📘 CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION (DETAILED VERSION)

1.1 Background of the Study



Over the past decade, India has undergone a major transformation in its financial and payment systems. Earlier, the economy was heavily dependent on cash transactions, especially in small cities and towns like Dhanbad. Small businesses such as kirana shops, vegetable vendors, tea stalls, and local service providers mainly relied on cash due to simplicity and lack of technological awareness.



However, with the introduction of digital payment systems like Unified Payments Interface (UPI), mobile wallets such as Paytm, and the rapid growth of smartphones and internet connectivity, a shift towards a cashless economy has taken place.



A major turning point came in 2016 with demonetization, when high-value currency notes were banned. This forced both customers and traders to explore digital alternatives. Later, during the COVID-19 pandemic, digital payments gained even more importance because people preferred contactless transactions for safety and hygiene reasons.



In cities like Dhanbad, which is known as the “Coal Capital of India,” the economy is a mix of industrial and small-scale businesses. Here, digital payments are not just a trend but are becoming a necessity. Small traders who once depended only on cash are now accepting payments through QR codes, mobile apps, and bank transfers.



Digital payments have made transactions faster, easier, and more transparent. At the same time, they have also introduced new challenges such as cyber fraud, lack of digital literacy, and dependence on internet connectivity. Therefore, it becomes important to study how these changes are affecting small businesses in real-life conditions.



1.2 Meaning and Concept of Digital Payments



Digital payments refer to the transfer of money from one person to another through electronic means without the use of physical cash. These transactions are completed using digital devices such as smartphones, computers, or point-of-sale (POS) machines.



In simple terms, when a customer pays a shopkeeper using a mobile app or card instead of cash, it is called a digital payment.



Key Features of Digital Payments:

Cashless Transactions – No need to carry physical money

Instant Transfer – Payments are completed within seconds

Secure Systems – Protected by passwords, OTPs, and encryption

Record Keeping – Automatic transaction history is maintained

Types of Digital Payment Systems

1. UPI (Unified Payments Interface)



UPI is one of the most popular digital payment systems in India. It allows users to transfer money directly from one bank account to another using a mobile app.



Examples:

Paytm UPI



👉 Features:

2. Mobile Wallets

3. Debit and Credit Cards

4. Net Banking



Online banking allows users to transfer money through bank websites.



👉 Features:

(1) Pre-Digital Phase (Before 2010)



Mostly cash-based economy

(2) Early Digital Phase (2010–2016)



Introduction of mobile banking



Growth of card payments



Emergence of mobile wallets

(3) Demonetization Phase (2016)

The 2016 Indian demonetization was a major turning point.

👉 Impact:





Sudden shortage of cash

Mobile wallets and apps gained popularity

(4) UPI Revolution Phase (2016–Present)

The launch of Unified Payments Interface changed everything.

Instant bank transfer



24×7 availability



No need for account details

Today, UPI is the backbone of India’s digital payment system.

Contactless payments increased



Digital adoption became necessity



Small vendors also shifted to QR payments
